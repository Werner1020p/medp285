Aquarius (Constellation)

            

► Aquarius can be difficult to spot with the naked eye. ◄

► Aquarius is a constellation in the Western Zodiac and one of 88 official constellations. Aquarius was said to represent a "cup bearer" or "water bearer," which is what its name means in Latin. And its old astronomical symbol is (♒︎), a representation of water. Aquarius is one of the oldest constellations documented in the historical record. ◄

► Aquarius is the 10th largest constellation in the sky according to astronomy. But there are no particularly bright stars in the constellation and it can be difficult to view with the naked eye. Even so, it covers about 980 square degrees and takes up more than two percent of the night sky when visible. ◄

► The constellation can be seen in the spring in the Southern Hemisphere and the fall in the Northern Hemisphere. ◄
